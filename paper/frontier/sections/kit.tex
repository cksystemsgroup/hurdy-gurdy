\section{The domain kit, and the far side as a design oracle}
\label{sec:kit}

Pointing the explorer at a domain costs four things: a reasoning hub
with a mechanized decision procedure and declared budgets;
deterministic shared interpreters for each language touched; at least
one external semantic anchor, if trust beyond per-run checking is
wanted; and a pinned benchmark. Given the kit, the theorems of
\Cref{sec:theorems} apply verbatim --- and the terminal board the
loop leaves behind is not a failure list but a design oracle: each
obstacle class carries an extraction operator turning its open
questions' evidence into the specification of the missing instrument,
exported as frontier pairs --- self-contained challenge bundles for
the domain's solver community.
