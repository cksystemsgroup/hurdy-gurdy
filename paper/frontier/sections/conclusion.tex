\section{Limitations and conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Four walls stand at every registry size --- undecidability, the
adequacy floor of the source semantics, worst-case decision cost, and
the finite supply of independent anchors --- and the theorems of
\Cref{sec:theorems} are drawn up to them, not past them. Within the
walls, saturation makes ``we don't know how to decide this'' a typed,
clustered, cost-profiled specification; the maps compound, because
pairs are shared.
