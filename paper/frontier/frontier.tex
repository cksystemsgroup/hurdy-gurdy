\begin{abstract}
%% Working abstract (Phase 1) — rewritten last, per the plan's writing
%% discipline. Two paragraphs on the v3-abstract model: the problem
%% and the theorems, then the instrument and the protocol.
Present a platform any benchmark whose questions reduce to decision
procedures. What would it mean for the platform to \emph{finish} the
benchmark --- and what architecture makes finishing detectable,
trustworthy, and portable across domains? We define
\emph{saturation}: every question either answered along every feasible
route, with measured cost and quantified trust, or open on the books
with its first failing obstacle, the evidence that would move it, and
the exact instrument --- a \emph{frontier pair}, a pair whose design
is unknown but whose required contract and design evidence are derived
mechanically --- that would close it, or the stated reason none can.
We prove that one architecture suffices: answerability is a monotone
filtration of candidate routes through five ordered conditions, so
exploration never loses ground, every open question has a unique
first-failing obstacle that only advances, and $N$ adequately targeted
extensions answer any question answerable at all --- relative
completeness in the lineage of Cook. The compositional core is
mechanized in Lean~4.

The instrument exists: \hg, a platform of deterministic,
fidelity-graded translation pairs with untrusted authors and measured
trust, is the means to this end, and this paper states the end. We
give the frontier problem, the explorer architecture, the facilitation
theorems with their honest assumptions, the domain kit any field must
supply, and the pre-registered saturation protocol for a benchmark
designed by others. This is a working draft: measurements are
deliberately absent --- the benchmarks section of this paper is the
saturation report its protocol will produce.
\end{abstract}
