\begin{abstract}
We are interested in constructing a platform that enables us to
explore the \emph{frontier of reducible decidability in practice} ---
which questions about programs can be answered, today, by chains of
sound reductions to mechanized decision procedures within declared
budgets --- and, eventually, to push that frontier. Saturating
benchmarks is just a way to get us there: present the platform a
pinned benchmark whose questions reduce to decision procedures, and
run it until every question is either answered along every feasible
route, with measured cost and quantified trust, or open on the books
with its first failing obstacle, the evidence that would move it, and
the exact missing instrument --- a \emph{frontier pair}, a pair whose
design is unknown but whose required contract and design evidence are
derived mechanically --- or the stated reason none can. Each
saturation deposits a map; the maps compound; and the terminal boards
are the demand side for the decision procedures that do the pushing.

\hg{} is that platform: deterministic, fidelity-graded translation
pairs with untrusted authors and measured
trust~\citep{hurdygurdy2026calculus}, read here as the means to the
map. We give the frontier problem (saturation, defined and made
mechanical), the explorer architecture, and six facilitation theorems
with their honest assumptions: answerability is a monotone filtration
of candidate routes through five ordered conditions, so exploration
never loses ground, every open question has a unique first-failing
obstacle that only advances, adequately targeted extensions answer
every question answerable at all --- relative completeness in the
lineage of Cook --- and per-benchmark saturation terminates
detectably. The compositional core is mechanized in Lean~4. We close
with the domain kit any field must supply and the pre-registered
saturation protocol for a benchmark designed by others; measurements
are deliberately absent --- the benchmarks section of this paper is
the saturation report its protocol will produce.
\end{abstract}
