\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

To answer a question about a program, move the program to where the
question is decidable. A C program's reachability question becomes a
bit-vector model-checking problem; a Python function's assertion becomes
a linear-arithmetic query; a chemical reaction network's coverability
question becomes an SMT unrolling. Every such move is a translation, and
every translation is a place to be wrong. The classical responses are to
\emph{prove} the translator once and for all
\citep{leroy2009compcert,kumar2014cakeml}, or to \emph{validate} each of
its runs \citep{pnueli1998tv,necula2000tv,lopes2021alive2}. Both are
statements about a single edge.\ifarxiv\repofootnote\fi

This paper is about the \emph{graph}. In practice the representations
worth reasoning in form a web: several source languages, a few
solver-facing targets, more than one way to get from here to there ---
and, crucially, edges of \emph{honestly different} trustworthiness. A
translator generated rule-for-rule from a logic-to-logic mapping is
foreseeable from its specification. An optimizing C compiler is not
foreseeable and not proved, only pinned. A translator written from an ISA
manual and one derived from the same ISA's formal model are neither
proved --- but they were produced \emph{independently}, and that
independence is itself a resource. A theory of the graph must let each
edge say what it actually guarantees, compose those statements honestly,
and show how several weak edges can corroborate one another into a strong
route. Its stance is the one distributed systems take toward processes:
a translator is a component that fails, and the failure is to be
\emph{contained} --- by declared loss, decidable checks, and independent
routes --- rather than prevented by universal proof.

\paragraph{An experiment in LLM-generated correctness, in both
directions}
This work is, deliberately, as much an experiment about \emph{who can
build and use} such a system as about the system itself. \hg\ --- the
platform this paper defines, builds, and measures --- exists to show
two things at once. First, that LLMs can be \emph{utilized} to
produce correct code: every pair --- translator, carry-back, tests ---
was implemented by an independent LLM agent from a one-page brief,
largely unsupervised, with the architecture's cross-checks as the only
semantic gate; the platform is the experiment's artifact, and
\S\ref{sec:bugs} reports what the gate caught. Second, that LLMs can
be \emph{supported} in producing correct conclusions: the platform's
intended player is an LLM, which chooses questions and routes but can
take no unchecked step --- routes of pairs hand the untrusted reasoner
deterministic moves whose trust is graded, cross-checked, and, for
witness-carrying answers, self-certifying at the source. This paper
evaluates the first direction throughout, and reports a first,
deliberately small controlled experiment on the second
(\S\ref{sec:eval-player}): LLM players answering ground-truthed
questions through the platform, every verdict carrying a
machine-checked evidence artifact where the unaided baseline offers
its say-so. Building the
instrument and playing it are the same thesis at two scales: trust
comes from the architecture, not from the author.\ifarxiv{} In the
limit the two directions are built to compose into a \emph{loop} ---
its closed-loop run still a concept, but its stages now shipped,
tested calls: an unanswerable question is diagnosed and the diagnosis
names the missing pair (\texttt{why-not}, \Cref{sec:overview}); unmet
demand accumulates in the platform's \emph{books} and is aggregated
into evidence-backed recommendations (\S\ref{sec:books}); a generator
drafts the brief, human registration admits it \emph{citing that
evidence}, the gate accepts or rejects the build, the ratchet
(\Cref{prop:ratchet}) keeps every prior verdict standing, and the
player re-asks. Run indefinitely, the
loop is a semi-algorithm for answerability itself: any question that
some finite chain of sound, deterministic reductions can bring to a
mechanized decision procedure is eventually answered, with quantified
trust --- in the limit, every \emph{reducibly decidable} question, at
fidelity that only rises, because a wrong generator cannot forge a
witness-carrying answer (\Cref{thm:existential}) and added routes
only corroborate. The limit is not an oracle: undecidability, the
adequacy floor, and the finite supply of independent semantic anchors
stand at every graph size (\Cref{sec:overview,sec:conclusion}).\fi{}

\paragraph{Provenance disclaimer}
All of \hg\ --- framework, shared interpreters, pairs, solver plumbing,
the evaluation harness, and the Lean mechanization --- is LLM-generated
code (Claude Opus and Claude Fable agents), produced largely
unsupervised under the discipline this paper describes; no human
reviewed the code for semantic correctness. Most of this paper's text
was likewise LLM-generated. The human contribution is the
architecture: the calculus, the contracts the agents were held to, and
the decisions about what to build and what to claim. Every
quantitative claim in \Cref{sec:evaluation} is regenerated from the
artifact by one script, and the mechanization's axiom audit is
re-checked at every build --- by design, the reader need not trust the
writers, human or otherwise, any further than the paper's own thesis
requires.

\paragraph{This paper}
We give the graph that theory, and measure an implementation of it. The
unit is the
\emph{pair} (\Cref{sec:calculus}): two formally defined languages, a pure
translator, pure interpreters for both languages (owned by the languages
and shared across all pairs touching them), and a pure
\emph{target-to-source interpreter} --- the component, usually an
afterthought, that carries a target-side behavior back to a source-side
behavior. The four functions close a commuting square, and the square
commuting \emph{at a given program} is decidable: run both sides, compare
under the pair's declared projection. A failure localizes to a step and a
named observable.

Squares compose. The pasting of commuting squares is folklore, and the
hypothesis it needs when each square commutes only \emph{up to a
projection} --- the earlier hop's carry-back must not read target
observables the later hop discards (\Cref{def:support,thm:pasting}) ---
is, as mathematics, a congruence condition whose cousins appear wherever
simulations compose vertically across differing observations
(\Cref{sec:related}). What we claim for it is its role as an explicit
design rule --- \emph{declare your loss} --- with a syntactic keep/loss
computation: a source observable survives a route iff no hop discards
what it depends on.

On this base we build the fidelity story (\Cref{sec:fidelity}). Each edge
declares a grade --- $\tpred$, $\trepr$, $\tchk$, $\tprov$, $\ttrust$ ---
naming its guarantee and evidence. Grades compose by weakest link, but on
an \emph{assurance-class} chain (universal / per-run / replay / none)
that separates the logic of a guarantee from the kind of evidence behind
it. Two further mechanisms move assurance where weakest-link says it
cannot go: \emph{re-establishment} (\Cref{thm:reestablish}) --- inline
square-checking lifts any single run over an opaque hop to per-run
faithfulness, which is how a pinned-but-unproved compiler can head a
verification route --- and \emph{branch corroboration}
(\Cref{lem:agree}) --- independently derived routes to the same target
that agree on the same input corroborate each other beyond either's
declared grade, with disagreement localizing to one hop of one route
(\Cref{lem:disagree}). A trusted-base ledger (\S\ref{sec:tcb}) itemizes
exactly what each layer of checking removes from the set of components
one must still believe.

The capstone (\S\ref{sec:endtoend}) is an asymmetry we believe deserves
to be a design principle. Answers that carry witnesses ---
\textsc{reachable}, \textsc{sat} --- are \emph{self-certifying at the
source}: carry the witness back through the route's target-to-source
interpreters and replay it in the source interpreter; soundness then
rests on interpreter adequacy alone, with every translator and solver
demoted to a discovery heuristic (\Cref{thm:existential}). Universal
answers --- \textsc{unreachable}, \textsc{unsat} --- admit no witness,
and are precisely where fidelity grades, branch agreement, and
independently checked certificates are the only currencies
(\Cref{thm:universal}). A translation architecture should therefore make
witness carry-back a first-class component of every edge --- cashing in
the free half of trust --- and spend its proof effort exclusively on the
other half.

\paragraph{Coverage, and honesty about partiality}
Fidelity is gameable by triviality (a one-instruction pair is vacuously
$\tprov$); coverage is gameable by unsoundness. We require their
per-construct \emph{conjunction} against spec-enumerated inventories
(\Cref{def:coverage}), with all rejection typed and enumerable, and we
prove the widening discipline sound: additive extensions preserve all
prior verdicts (\Cref{prop:ratchet}), so coverage ratchets monotonically
over a project's history.

\paragraph{The instantiation}
The calculus is implemented as \hg, a platform of 13 registered languages
and 13 pairs (\Cref{sec:instantiation}): C, RISC-V, AArch64, Wasm, eBPF,
EVM, Python, Sail, chemical reaction networks, SMILES molecular graphs
with their molecular-formula target,
and two reasoning hubs --- BTOR2 (bit-level) and SMT-LIB (theory-level) ---
plus dual, independently derived routes RISC-V$\to$BTOR2 and
AArch64$\to$BTOR2 (manual-derived vs.\ Sail-model-derived), each pair
agent-built as framed above with the square oracle, external
differentials (a Sail-generated ISA simulator, a pinned CPython), solver
corroboration, and branch cross-checks as the only police.
\Cref{sec:evaluation} reports the resulting capability snapshot ---
per-pair conjoined coverage, composed route coverage, branch-agreement
runs down to SMT-LIB, end-to-end case studies with source-level witness
replay, certified unreachability, performance, and the defects the
architecture caught along the way, each localized as
\Cref{cor:localization} promises.\ifarxiv{} (Fifteen pairs are
registered in all: the snapshot's thirteen plus the two abstraction
endo-pairs of the direction axis --- \texttt{btor2-havoc} built,
\texttt{btor2-interval} a registered brief; \S\ref{sec:lax},
\Cref{sec:instantiation}.)\fi{}

\paragraph{Contributions}
\begin{itemize}
\item A calculus of pairs: commuting squares with declared projections,
  decidable per-program checking with step/observable localization, and a
  pasting theorem whose projection-compatibility hypothesis makes
  cumulative loss compositional (\Cref{sec:calculus}).
\ifarxiv
\item The square as a \emph{directional} claim from the start: pairs
  declare a direction and witness embedding, exactness is the
  identity-embedding special case (\Cref{prop:exactid}), universal
  verdicts transfer across over-approximation (\Cref{prop:lax}), and
  abstraction enters the platform as a registered, negative-controlled
  pair whose refinement loop is CEGAR with durable artifacts
  (\S\ref{sec:lax}) --- the lax telescope mechanized with the same
  axiom audit as the exact one (\S\ref{sec:mech}).
\fi
\item A fidelity algebra: grades vs.\ assurance classes, weakest-link
  composition, per-run re-establishment, branch corroboration under an
  explicit diversity assumption, and a trusted-base ledger
  (\Cref{sec:fidelity}) --- with the compositional core of both
  sections mechanized in Lean~4 (\S\ref{sec:mech}).
\item The existential/universal asymmetry as an architectural principle,
  with the end-to-end soundness theorems that express it
  (\S\ref{sec:endtoend}).
\ifarxiv
\item The loop's accounting, shipped: one ledger keeping measured cost
  and recorded demand, an answerability diagnosis whose failures name
  the missing pair, and a \emph{recommended-then-registered} discipline
  --- no evidence, no brief; registration stays human --- extending the
  untrusted-generator stance from building pairs to choosing them
  (\S\ref{sec:books}).
\fi
\item A measured, LLM-built instantiation: 13 agent-built pairs over
  13 languages and two solver hubs, evaluated for conjoined coverage,
  branch agreement --- including decision stacks fully disjoint after
  the head --- a compliance-derived benchmark with machine-derived
  ground truth, end-to-end witness replay, certified unreachability
  re-validated by a formally verified checker at up-to-megabyte
  certificate scale, measured escape rates for the gate stack ---
  single-leg and common-mode, the latter finding and then closing a
  full-stack escape --- a
  first controlled LLM-player experiment, and the defects caught
  (\Cref{sec:instantiation,sec:evaluation}).
\end{itemize}
